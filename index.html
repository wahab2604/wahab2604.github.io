About Me

Combining research capabilities with practical industrial applications

I am a Ph.D. chemical engineer with a solid chemistry foundation and experience in both academic and commercial research environments.

As an experienced cement chemist, I specialize in thermodynamic modeling, high-temperature synthesis, and mechanical testing. My work combines strong research capabilities with practical industrial applications and projects.

I have a proven track record in developing innovative cement solutions, managing research projects, and creating computational tools that bridge fundamental science with real-world applications.

Research Experience

From doctoral research to leading industrial cement projects

Principal Researcher

Gulf Organisation for Research and Development, Doha, Qatar

May 2025 - Present

  • Lead cement chemist responsible for all cement activities at the institute internally and externally funded.
  • Manage clinker synthesis lab and built a mechanical testing lab.
  • Led development of AI-based process optimisation software for clinker production, currently in trial with Qatar National Cement Company.
  • Synthesised and tested mortar samples containing a novel nanomaterial admixture, showing up to 30% improvement in mechanical strength.
  • Created financial models for three spin-out technologies with corresponding pitch decks.
  • Led and submitted proposals for calls from Qatar Research Development and Innovation Council.

Post Doctoral Researcher

Gulf Organisation for Research and Development, Doha, Qatar

May 2024 - May 2025

  • Contributed to large scale project funded by QRDI (~3M USD), developing a cost-effective CSA cement from materials provided by Qatar National Cement Company.
  • Contributed to a Qatar-Spain project (~0.5M USD) - carried out DFT to screen metal complexes for gas-sensing applications.
  • Synthesised and characterised low ye'elimite and reactive belite CSA clinkers using a novel sulfur combustion process.
  • Produced cost competitive cement clinker at pilot scale (500 kg) - equivalent performance to Portland at 28 days with much lower GWP.

Post Doctoral Researcher

University of Sheffield, Sheffield, UK

Mar 2023 - Apr 2024

  • Contributed to large scale project funded by UKRI (~4M USD), developing the manufacture of BCSA cements from waste materials.
  • Modelled new thermodynamic properties of main cement clinker phases, secondary phases, and the effect of impurities.
  • Developed tools and techniques enabling pilot trial producing 1 ton of material.
  • Developed process models for CSA cement manufacture, including thermodynamic, kinetic and heat transfer effects.

Doctoral Researcher

University of Aberdeen, Aberdeen, UK

Sep 2019 - Feb 2023

  • International project funded by Nanocem (industrial cement consortium) researching thermodynamics of Portland cement clinkers.
  • Applied the CALPHAD approach to assess thermodynamic properties of cement clinker phases including minor elements.
  • Developed Python tools and novel modelling techniques for phase diagram assessment.
  • Validated developed thermodynamic database against real clinkers received from industry.
